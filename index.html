DVA1501

Development Studies I

Sustainable development, theories & policy

▼
📅 Exam: 15 May — Moodle Proctored
  • Development improves human wellbeing: economic, social, political and cultural.
  • Sustainable Development (Brundtland 1987): present needs without compromising future generations.
  • 3 Pillars: Economic growth + Social equity + Environmental protection.
  • Modernisation Theory (Rostow): linear stages from traditional to modern.
  • Dependency Theory: underdevelopment caused by exploitation by rich nations.
  • HDI measures life expectancy, education, and per capita income.
  • 17 SDGs adopted by UN in 2015, targeting 2030.
DVA1502

Development Studies II

Gender, 4IR, NHI, participatory development

▼
📅 Exam: 23 May
  • Gender mainstreaming: integrating gender equality into all policies and programmes.
  • 4IR: AI, robotics, IoT and big data transforming economies.
  • NHI: SA's plan for universal health coverage.
  • Participatory development: communities actively involved in decisions.
  • Paulo Freire: education as liberation, raising critical consciousness.
  • Conscientisation: critical awareness leading to social action.
PLC1502

Public Law & Legislation

SA Constitution, rights, governance

▼
📅 Exam: 1 July
  • Constitution (1996) is the supreme law of South Africa.
  • Bill of Rights (Chapter 2): equality, dignity, freedom of expression.
  • Separation of powers: Legislature + Executive + Judiciary.
  • 3 spheres: National + Provincial + Local (cooperative governance).
  • Section 195: public admin must be democratic, accountable, transparent.
  • PAJA: ensures fair and lawful administrative action.
INF1505

Information Systems

Computing, networks, databases

▼
  • Information System: collects, processes, stores and outputs data.
  • Hardware = physical. Software = programs and applications.
  • Networks: LAN (Local), WAN (Wide), MAN (Metropolitan).
  • DBMS organises and manages structured data.
  • Internet = public global. Intranet = private internal.
EUP1501

End-User Computing

Excel, Word, formulas & spreadsheets

▼
  • Excel formulas start with '='. Example: =SUM(A1:A10).
  • Nested IF: =IF(condition, value_if_true, IF(condition2, value2, value3))
  • VLOOKUP searches a column and returns data from another column.
  • Absolute $A$1 stays fixed. Relative A1 adjusts when copying.
  • Charts: select data → Insert → choose chart type.
IOP1501

Intro to Psychology

Behaviour, motivation, personality

▼
  • Psychology: scientific study of behaviour and mental processes.
  • Maslow: Physiological → Safety → Love → Esteem → Self-actualisation.
  • Classical conditioning (Pavlov): learning through stimulus association.
  • Operant conditioning (Skinner): reinforcement and punishment shape behaviour.
  • Big Five traits: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism.

Harvard Referencing
UNISA standard — cite correctly every time

📖 Book / Study Guide

Reference List

Surname, Initials. Year. Title of book. Edition. Place: Publisher.
Dlamini, S.B. 2023. Public Administration in South Africa. 3rd ed. Pretoria: Van Schaik.

In-text Citation

(Surname Year:page) → e.g. (Dlamini 2023:45)

🌐 Website / Online

Author/Organisation. Year. Page title. [Online]. Available: URL [Accessed: Day Month Year].
UNISA. 2024. Student registration guide. [Online]. Available: https://www.unisa.ac.za [Accessed: 5 May 2025].

📰 Journal Article

Surname, Initials. Year. Article title. Journal Name. Volume(Issue):pages.
Nkosi, T. 2022. Gender equality in SA development. African Dev Review. 14(1):22–38.

✅ Golden Rules

  • Reference list is alphabetical by author surname.
  • Every in-text citation must have a matching reference list entry.
  • Direct quotes need page numbers and quotation marks.
  • Paraphrase to reduce Turnitin — same meaning, your own words.
  • Never use a URL as an in-text citation.
  • UNISA study guides: cite as (UNISA Year:page).
